# Participant Information and Consent Form
**Study:** Conversational Clustering on Conflict Event Data  
**Version:** v1.0  
**Status:** Locked — do not modify after the first rater completes the form.

---

## Purpose of the study

This study investigates whether an AI language model can reliably evaluate the quality of data clustering — specifically, whether its judgements agree with human judgements. You are being asked to provide the human judgements that we will compare the AI against.

The study is conducted as part of a university course project. It is not funded by any external party and has no commercial purpose.

---

## What participation involves

You will complete a pairwise preference task: for each of 20 pairs of cluster descriptions, you choose which grouping of conflict events makes more sense as a meaningful category. Full instructions are in `docs/rater_instructions.md`.

**Time required:** approximately 15–20 minutes.  
**Content:** Descriptions of real military conflict events (Russia-Ukraine war, 2022–2024), including references to casualties. No graphic imagery is shown.

---

## Voluntary participation

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may stop at any time without giving a reason and without any consequence. If you stop partway through, any responses you have already submitted will be included in the analysis unless you request otherwise.

---

## Data collected

The rating form collects:
- Your forced-choice preference for each pair (Option A or B)
- Optional free-text comments per pair
- A randomly assigned anonymous rater ID (generated at form entry)

**No names, email addresses, or other identifying information are collected.**

---

## How data is stored and used

- Responses are stored in the project repository in anonymised form (rater ID only).
- Only aggregate statistics (majority vote, inter-rater agreement) will be reported in any publication or course submission.
- No individual-level data will be published or shared outside the research team.
- Data will be retained for the duration of the course and may be included in the project's replication package.
